CASE ID: 3348dab0-31d0-470e-b0e3-1804afd25f69

1. SUMMARY OF THE SCAM
The 1992 Indian securities scam was a market manipulation orchestrated by stockbroker Harshad Mehta. He exploited loopholes in the banking system, specifically the Ready Forward (RF) deal system and Bank Receipts (BRs), to divert massive funds from public sector banks to the stock market.

2. CORE ENTITIES
- HARSHAD MEHTA: Primary orchestrator, known as "The Big Bull".
- GROWMORE RESEARCH & ASSET MANAGEMENT: Mehta's firm.
- STATE BANK OF INDIA (SBI): Source of significant diverted funds (approx. ₹4,000 crore).
- NATIONAL HOUSING BANK (NHB): Heavily involved in the BR irregularities.
- BOMBAY STOCK EXCHANGE (BSE): The primary arena for the speculation.

3. KEY RELATIONSHIPS

- GROWMORE -> transacts_with -> BOMBAY STOCK EXCHANGE
